Copy/Paste Clipboard Ace

Support & Help

Getting Started

  • After launching, the app lives in your Mac’s menu bar near the clock.
  • Copy text, images, or files to add them to your clipboard history.
  • Click any item in the list to reuse it.
  • Lock important items so they stay available.

Enabling Auto-Paste

Copy/Paste Clipboard Ace can paste items directly into your active app with one click. This requires macOS Accessibility permission.

  • Open System Settings on your Mac.
  • Go to Privacy & Security → Accessibility.
  • Find Copy/Paste Clipboard Ace and turn it on.
  • If it is not listed, add it from your Applications folder.

Without this permission, items are still copied to your clipboard and can be pasted manually with ⌘V.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my clipboard data sent anywhere?

No. Clipboard data stays on your Mac and is not uploaded or shared.

How do I stop clipboard monitoring?

Open Preferences → Privacy and turn off Monitor clipboard automatically.

How do I delete my clipboard history?

Open Preferences → Privacy and click Clear all clipboard history.

What is Skip Sensitive Content?

When enabled, the app attempts to skip passwords, credit cards, and social security numbers. It is enabled by default but may not detect everything.

How do I use keyboard shortcuts?

The default shortcuts are ⌥V to paste the top item and ⇧⌥V to paste all items. You can change these in Preferences → Shortcuts.
