Copy/Paste Clipboard Ace

Support & Help

Getting Started

  • After launching, the app lives in your Mac's menu bar — look for the clipboard icon near the clock in the top-right corner.
  • Copy anything — text, images, files — and it appears in the list automatically.
  • Click any item in the list to paste it instantly into your active app.
  • Lock an item by clicking the lock icon on the left to keep it in your list permanently.

Enabling Auto-Paste

Copy/Paste Clipboard Ace can paste items directly into your active app with one click. This requires macOS Accessibility permission.

  • Open System Settings on your Mac.
  • Go to Privacy & Security → Accessibility.
  • Find Copy/Paste Clipboard Ace in the list and toggle it on.
  • If the app isn't listed yet, click the + button and add it from your Applications folder.

Without this permission, items are still copied to your clipboard — you can paste them manually with ⌘V.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is my clipboard data sent anywhere?

No. Everything stays on your Mac. No data is uploaded, transmitted, or shared with anyone — ever. You can verify this in Preferences → Privacy.

How do I stop the app from monitoring my clipboard?

Open Preferences → Privacy and toggle off Monitor clipboard automatically. You can also pause it temporarily from the same screen.

How do I delete my clipboard history?

Open Preferences → Privacy → Clear all clipboard history. This removes all items including locked ones. History is also automatically cleared when you quit the app.

The app icon isn't in my menu bar.

Make sure the app is running. If it still doesn't appear, your menu bar may be full — try holding ⌘ and dragging some icons to make space, or check System Settings → Control Centre.

How do I use the keyboard shortcut to paste?

The default shortcuts are ⌥V to paste the top item and ⇧⌥V to paste all items. You can customize these in Preferences → Shortcuts.

How do I split copied lines into individual items?

Tap the split icon ⇥ in the main window header, or enable List copied lines (split by line breaks) individually in Preferences → General.

What is "Skip sensitive content"?

When enabled (on by default), the app attempts to detect and skip passwords, credit cards, and social security numbers. It's not foolproof — avoid copying highly sensitive data into any clipboard manager.

How do I lock an item so it stays in the list?

Hover over any item and click the lock icon on the left side of the row. Locked items appear at the bottom of the list and survive Clear All and app restarts.

The app pasted a √ character instead of my item.

This can happen if your keyboard shortcut uses ⌥V alone without proper interception. Go to Preferences → Shortcuts and reassign the shortcut to a combo that includes ⌃ (Control), such as ⌃⌥V.

How do I remove the app from Accessibility in System Settings?

Open System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility, find Copy/Paste Clipboard Ace, and click the − button to remove it. The app will still work — auto-paste will simply be disabled.
